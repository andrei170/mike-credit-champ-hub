The Leak Points

Three Places The Funnel Currently Underconverts

All publicly observable on mikethecreditchamp.com and the @mikethecreditchamp feed. Nothing fabricated about backend systems we can't see.

Critical

Dual Audience Runs As One Funnel

The homepage serves two audiences with one funnel. The consumer who landed because their FICO is at 545 and they want it at 700 is hitting the same primary CTA ("BOOK A CONSULTATION") as the entrepreneur who landed because they want to launch or scale a credit-repair business. Different intent, different price point, different sales cycle. Currently both routes through "BOOK A CONSULTATION" and the "Credit Repair Services" / "Mentorship & Sponsorship" navigation links, with no audience self-sort at the homepage entry. The consumer drops because the page also talks to entrepreneurs. The entrepreneur drops because the page leads with credit repair services. Both audiences are paying a UX tax for the cross-pollination.

The LiftAn audience-routing layer at the homepage entry. Two clearly-marked paths off the hero ("Fix My Credit" / "Build A Credit Business") routing into separate landing pages with audience-specific copy, audience-specific VSLs, and audience-specific lead magnets. Same brand, two funnels under it, each converting to its own intent.
Critical

No Premium Tier Above The Mentorship

The Mentorship is the current entrepreneur flagship. Application-only, branded as "Mentor Me Mike," covering LLC setup, credit building, Toro and Airbnb business modules, direct coaching, and networking access. Solid offer, real students. And it's where the entrepreneur ladder ends. There is no $15K-$20K cohort tier for mentees who have launched, hit $50K-$100K/mo in their credit-repair or multi-line business, and are positioned to scale to $1M+. With 301K Instagram audience and a four-brand operating footprint, you sit above almost every other credit-industry educator running a single mentorship tier. Daniel Rosen at Credit Repair Cloud runs a VIP Mastermind gated by "150+ clients served." That's the senior tier shape. Yours has been earned. It's just not built. The Mentorship alumni base is the warmest possible list for a senior tier, and currently those graduates either churn back into DM contact or get left without a structured upgrade path.

The LiftThe Credit Operator Cohort. Quarterly intakes of 6-8 mature operators (1+ launched credit-repair or income-stack business, $50K-$100K/mo revenue, often Mentorship alumni). $15K-$20K per seat, two-day in-person Dallas intake plus 90-day implementation. Sells first off your existing Mentorship alumni base. Easier to fill than a 25-seat room. Produces case studies that compound back into Mentorship marketing.
High Impact

No VSL On Either Audience Path

Both audiences hit a text-heavy homepage with three named testimonials and a contact form, but no video. Consumer credit-repair is high-skepticism: most prospects have already been pitched five times by less-credentialled operators or burned by a previous service. The consumer needs an 8-10 minute walkthrough showing the dashboard, the dispute process, the Next Level Credit Plan timeline, and the named outcomes. The entrepreneur needs an 8-12 minute walkthrough showing real mentee unit economics, the LLC-to-cash-flow path, and what the post-Mentorship tier looks like. Currently neither audience gets the video pre-frame, so both convert at cold-traffic baseline rather than warmed-traffic baseline.

The LiftTwo VSLs, one per audience path, embedded above the application gate on each routed landing. Consumer VSL: the Next Level Credit Plan walkthrough plus dashboard footage plus named-outcome screenshots. Operator VSL: the Mentorship-to-Cohort path plus mentee unit economics plus the multi-line operator framing.

Questions

The Ones An Operator At Your Stage Asks

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the business. The Next Level Credit Plan is fulfilling. The Mentorship is delivering. The Credit Repair Services line is booking consultations. Adding a 90-day audience-routed funnel build, two VSLs, a premium-tier launch, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded ops team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

Roughly half a day to record the consumer VSL. One day to record the operator VSL plus the Credit Operator Cohort positioning content. Calendar holds for cohort delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

Will the Credit Operator Cohort cannibalise the Mentorship?

No. The Cohort and the Mentorship solve different problems. The Mentorship is a structured curriculum for entrepreneurs entering the credit-and-income-line space. The Cohort is strategic operating-room access for operators who already run a launched business at $50K-$100K/mo and want to scale to $1M+. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. Most Cohort applicants will be Mentorship graduates moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Cohort over Mentorship. Where there's overlap, the Cohort is the explicit "post-Mentorship" tier. It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

How does this work alongside my current team?

The build team operates alongside your existing structure. Your customer success team keeps running the Next Level Credit Plan dispute timeline. Your Mentorship delivery stays where it is. Your Credit Repair Services bookings keep flowing. We layer on top: audience-routed homepage, dual VSL, segmented nurture, Cohort sales engine, paid layer, alumni community. The only meaningful interface point is Mike on camera for the two VSLs and the Cohort positioning content. Everything else, we run autonomously and report back.

What if my existing Mentorship already covers some of what the Cohort would?

There's overlap on some content (LLC structure, credit fundamentals, business mechanics), but the deliverable is different. The Mentorship is "we teach you how to launch and operate a credit-and-income-line business." The Cohort is "you already operate one at $50K-$100K/mo, here's how to scale to $1M+ with peer operators in a strategic format the Mentorship can't deliver." Most Cohort attendees will have already passed through the Mentorship and outgrown the entry format. The overlap is feature, not bug. Cohort buys insider continuity for operators who don't want to start from scratch with another mentor.

Lever 03 paid layer starts producing measurable ROAS by month 4. Lever 04 alumni community ramps from month 4 onward. Most of the year-one incremental lands in months 4-12, not month one.

What if the Cohort doesn't fill?

The Cohort is sized at 6-8 seats first, sold to existing Mentorship alumni who've launched (the warmest list for this offer that exists). If the first intake under-fills (say, 4 confirmed instead of 6), we don't push it into a half-empty room. We pause, sharpen the positioning and the application screen, push the date by 30-45 days, and try again with the second wave of alumni outreach plus any external warm sources we can verify. Build doesn't continue billing while the Cohort isn't running. If after a second attempt the audience isn't there at the modelled price, we restructure the tier or move the budget into Lever 03 paid acquisition.

What if I want to stop after a Lever?

You can stop at any Lever. After Lever 01 you own the audience-routed homepage, the two VSLs, the lead magnets, and the segmented nurture as standalone lifts on your existing funnel. After Lever 02 you own the Cohort sales page, application flow, 90-day curriculum, and the first cohort revenue. After Lever 03 you own the paid acquisition system. Each Lever stands on its own as a fully shipped asset in case the rest stops being interesting to you.
